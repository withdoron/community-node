**a. Local Government Assessments:** All local government assessments (i.e. sewer, street, water, Systems Development Charges, etc.) are the responsibility of the Owner unless otherwise specified in this contract.

**b. Non-binding Items:** Any specifications, drawings, letters, pricing, or conversation between Owner and Contractor prior to contract acceptance or during completion of project (with the exception of Change Orders) are to be used as guides only and are not binding upon Contractor.

**c. Decision-making Authority:** When dealing with more than one Owner, any decision made by any individual Owner is binding on all other Owners.

**d.** In connection with remodeling or reconstruction projects, any ballpark estimates or price ranges that are provided are based solely on observations the Contractor was able to make with the structure in its current condition at the time. If additional concealed conditions are discovered once work has commenced which were not visible at the time this proposal was made, Contractor will point out these unforeseen concealed conditions to the Owner so that the Owner can make any decisions necessary for project completion.

**e.** Any material replacement, structural requirements, or work required by governmental building inspectors which has not be specifically identified and which are required to complete the job, will be recognized as additional cost to this Contract. Contractor shall notify Owner in writing within 30 days of any required change. **After consultation with the Contractor, any changes beyond the scope of the building plans must be approved by the Owner.**

**f. Hazardous Materials:** If, during the course of work, the Contractor encounters hazardous materials the Contractor will cease work immediately. The Owner is responsible for hazardous material removal and for payment of Contractor's services up to the date work ceases.

**g.** When plans are provided by others any design deficiencies or omissions in the plans and specifications requiring additional costs by Contractor and which are necessary for job completion will be recognized as an approved Change Order to this Contract. Contractor shall notify Owner of any such changes in writing within 30 days of cost incurrence.

**h. Contractor's Warranty:** The warranties in this paragraph are in lieu of other warranties. There are no other warranties, express or implied, of merchantability or fitness for any purpose. Contractor warrants that all materials and equipment furnished by it will be new and of good quality, unless specified otherwise, and will be installed or applied in a good and workmanlike manner. Contractor will, at its option, either repair or replace defective work provided under this Contract for a period of one (1) year from completion of the Work. Contractor's duty to repair or replace is conditional upon Owner's compliance in full with payments terms and other conditions of this Contract and upon written notice to Contractor of any such defect. Contractor shall not be liable for special, indirect or consequential damages and liability hereunder is limited to replacement or corrections of defective materials or labor provided under this Contract. Contractor shall not be responsible for work done or repairs made outside this Contract. This warranty shall not apply to fixtures, equipment or appliances that are warranted by their respective manufacturers or distributors. Such manufacturer's warranties shall be deemed to run to the benefit of the Owner and are hereby assigned by Contractor to Owner.

**i. Owner's Obligations:** Owner shall clear the immediate work area and any adjacent areas that may be impacted by the Work of furnishings and personal property before the Work is scheduled to begin. Contractor shall have no liability for damage to such items which are not removed or to lawns and landscaping resulting from Contractor's reasonable construction practices.

**j. Liens:** Contractor will keep premises free from all construction liens by reason of any material or labor to be furnished to Contractor by others; *provided,* that abides by the above Payment Terms. The owner may request evidence that all payrolls, material bill and other indebtedness connected with the work have been paid. Nothing shall prevent the Contractor from timely filing a construction lien on his own behalf to secure payment of the money to become due under this Contract should he deem it necessary.

**k. Work Direction/Supervision:** Owner understands and agrees that Contractor has total responsibility for job and for any errors, omissions, faulty workmanship, etc. Owner shall direct all questions, directions, etc. to Contractor or Contractor's representative and shall not direct or attempt to supervise any employees or subcontractors performing work for Contractor. Owner shall not make any arrangements for additional work on the job by others without prior approval by Contractor.

**l. Utilities and Storage:** Contractor and its agents and employees shall have free use of utilities and facilities necessary to complete the Work, including but not limited to electrical power, water, telephone, and toilets. The owner shall provide safe and secure storage for materials and equipment at the job site without charge.

**m.** Upon request, Contractor will furnish Owners with material, appliance and fixture specifications used by Contractor or subcontractor to ensure use of Owner-requested material, appliances and fixtures.

**n.** Should either party desire to terminate this contract, Contractor shall be paid for all work to date. Once a Mutual Dissolution of Contract is signed, neither party shall have further recourse against the other.

**o.** If there is more than one named Owner, the obligation of each shall be joint and several. All terms and conditions herein contained shall apply and inure to the benefit of and bind the heirs, executors, administrators, successors and assigns of the respective parties hereto. In this Contract, the singular includes the plural. Each of the undersigned promises and agrees to pay the reasonable collection costs of the holder thereof; however, if suit or action including Construction Contractors Board proceedings or arbitration is filed hereon, the prevailing party therein that recover (1) his reasonable attorney's fees to be fixed by the trial court and (2) if any appeal is taken from any decision of the trial court, such further sum as may be fixed by the appellate court, as the prevailing party's reasonable attorney's fees in the appellate court.
