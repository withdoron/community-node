### Section 2. Payment

Contractor agrees to pay Subcontractor for the full and faithful performance of Subcontractor's performance here under the Subcontract Amount, in good funds, subject only to the following express conditions:

**2.1 Progress Payments.** The subcontractor shall submit progress payment invoices as set forth in Section 1.3 of this Subcontract. Invoices received after this deadline will be paid the following month. Contractor's duty to pay invoices received more than seventy-five (75) days following Subcontractor's completion of the Work is conditioned upon Contractor's receipt of payment, attributable to the Work, from the Owner.

**2.2 Satisfactory Work.** No payment shall be made for substandard workmanship or materials, as determined in the Owner's and Contractor's reasonable discretion.

**2.3 Creditor Payments.** Subcontractors shall make timely payments to its employees, suppliers, subcontractors, or other Project-related creditors or employees. If Subcontractor fails to do so, Contractor or Owner may thereafter pay Subcontractor's invoices by means of two-party checks.

**2.4 Insurance.** Contractor's duty to pay invoices is conditioned upon Contractor's receipt of Subcontractor's certificates for the insurance described in Section 10.

### Section 3. Change Orders

Contractor may require additions to or omissions from the Work, as a consequence of which the Subcontract Amount shall be adjusted so as not to cause substantial inequity to either Subcontractor or Contractor. Before proceeding with any change to the Work, the Subcontractor shall first obtain written authorization from the Contractor, or, in the case of field authorization of change orders, as soon thereafter as is practicable. The terms of change orders shall become a part of this Agreement.

### Section 4. Subcontractor Communications

Subcontractor shall deal solely with Contractor (rather than Owner) regarding any aspect of the Work or the Project, including without limitation change in the Work.

### Section 5. Risk of Loss

Subcontractor assumes all risk of loss, damage or destruction to Subcontractor's materials, tools, and equipment.

### Section 6. Completion Time

Time is of the essence of this Subcontract. Before commencement of the Work, Subcontractor and Contractor shall establish a schedule for completion of the Work ("Work Timeline"). The subcontractor shall furnish all the information required by Contractor necessary to prepare, update and expedite the Work Timeline. The subcontractor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to abide by the Work Timeline, as may be mutually amended. The Work Timeline will be extended for any delay in the progress of the Work not caused by the Subcontractor. Such an extension will be Subcontractor's only remedy for delays not caused by Subcontractor. Subcontractor shall coordinate its Work with that of all other contractors and subcontractors in a manner that will facilitate timely completion of the Project.

### Section 7. Access and Use of the Construction Sites

During the performance of the Work, Subcontractor, its employees, subcontractors and suppliers shall use only those entrances to the Project designated by Contractor. The subcontractor shall ensure that its suppliers make Project deliveries when and where designated by Contractor. Subcontractor shall provide, at its own expense, whatever storage sheds, trailers, workshops and offices as may be necessary for the performance of the Work, which shall be located at places designated by Contractor and promptly removed upon completion of the Work.

### Section 8. Waivers of Lien/Claims of Suppliers and Subcontractors

Upon Contractor's reasonable request, Subcontractor shall furnish effectively executed releases or waivers of liens and claims, whether on behalf of Subcontractor or its suppliers, subcontractors and Project-related creditors: (a) in connection with completed portions of the Work; and (b) in exchange for final payment of the Subcontract Amount when the Work has been completed.

### Section 9. Indemnification

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Subcontractor shall indemnify and hold harmless Contractor, its agents, employees, representatives or subcontractors from all liability, damages, losses, claims and expenses (including reasonable attorneys fees, collection costs, and administrative expenses) resulting, or having allegedly resulted, from Subcontractor's actions or omissions. This indemnity shall not apply to liabilities, damages, losses, claims and expenses caused solely by the acts or omissions of Contractor, its agents, employees, representatives, or other subcontractors.

### Section 10. Insurance Requirements

The subcontractor shall purchase and keep in full force until one year after final payment the following insurance:

**10.1** Workers Compensation and Employer's Liability Insurance if and as prescribed by law.

**10.2** Commercial General Liability Bodily Injury and Property Damage Insurance written on an "occurrence" basis. The limits of the liability for this insurance shall not be less than the coverage limits required of the Contractor by Owner, with Contractor included as an additional insured thereunder.

### Section 11. Safety Compliance

The subcontractor shall strictly enforce safety requirements with respect to the Work, and shall install and maintain all safety appliances, railings, supports, shoring, guards and other devices necessary to assure safety. If Subcontractor fails in this regard, then Contractor may, at its option but without any responsibility to do so, provide necessary safety at Subcontractor's expense.

### Section 12. Acceptance of Conditions; Notice before Proceeding with Work

Subcontractor represents that it (a) has visited the jobsite and is fully familiar with the conditions under which the Work is to be performed, (b) has determined that the conditions are suitable for performance of the Work, and (c) has included fully accounted for any allowances for the conditions in the Subcontract Amount. If Subcontractor later determines that jobsite conditions have become unsatisfactory or unsuitable for the Work after performance has commenced, then Subcontractor shall promptly notify Contractor before proceeding with the Work or taking remedial action. Failure to notify Contractor promptly shall constitute Subcontractor's acceptance of the jobsite conditions, as a consequence of which Subcontractor shall not be entitled to extra compensation but shall instead be liable for defects in the Work resulting from the jobsite condition.

### Section 13. Equipment and Materials

Without the prior written consent of Contractor, all equipment, materials or supplies that Subcontractor places or installs in the Project shall be solely owned by Subcontractor, in new and good condition, and not subject to any lien or encumbrance (except those arising from a standard flooring arrangement).

### Section 14. Assignment

Subcontractor shall not assign any rights or delegate any duties under this Subcontract without first obtaining the written consent of Contractor. Assignments of rights or delegations of duties in violation of this section are VOID.

### Section 15. Clean Up

Subcontractor shall clean up and remove from the site on a regular basis all rubbish and debris resulting from the Work, including dirt, grease, marks and the like from walls, ceilings, floors, and fixtures. If Subcontractor refuses or fails in this regard, then Contractor shall have the right to remedy the failure at Subcontractor's expense, which shall include the actual costs thereof plus fifteen percent (25%) to cover supervision, insurance, overhead, and the like.

### Section 16. Independent Contractor

Subcontractor is an independent contractor, and nothing in this Subcontract shall be construed to create a joint venture, principal-agent, partnership, employer-employee or other relationship between Contractor and Subcontractor. The subcontractor shall be solely responsible for directing, supervising and bearing all costs associated with its employees and agents.

**SUBCONTRACTOR IS AN INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AND IS SOLELY RESPONSIBLE FOR PAYMENT OF ALL FEDERAL, STATE, COUNTY, AND ANY OTHER APPLICABLE TAXES AND INSURANCE AND CONTRIBUTIONS FOR SOCIAL SECURITY AND UNEMPLOYMENT TAXES WHICH ARE BASED ON THE WAGES, SALARIES OR OTHER COMPENSATION PAID BY SUBCONTRACTOR TO ITS EMPLOYEES UNDER THIS SUBCONTRACT OR OTHERWISE.**

### Section 17. Warranty

For a period of one (1) year from the date of Substantial Completion of the Project as defined in Contractor's agreement with Owner, Subcontractor warrants that the Work shall be of good quality and free from defective workmanship, and that all equipment and materials used shall be appropriate for the Work and installed in accordance with the manufacturer's specifications. If Subcontractor installs any material or equipment in a manner that voids the manufacturer's warranty without prior written authorization of Contractor, then Subcontractor shall, at its expense, assume the obligations of the manufacturer's warranty, irrespective of whether the warranty work is beyond the scope of the original Work.

### Section 18. Default

The following shall be an "Event of Default" under this Subcontract: (a) Subcontractor is the subject of a petition for bankruptcy, or other similar action indicating Subcontractor's insolvency; (b) in connection with the Work, Subcontractor fails to make prompt payment to employees, laborers, subcontractors or suppliers; (c) Subcontractor fails to execute the Work properly and diligently; (d) Subcontractor fails promptly and properly to remedy any "punch list" item allocated to Subcontractor by Contractor; (e) Subcontractor fails promptly to correct any defective or non-conforming Work, or fails to adhere to its warranty obligations as set forth in Section 17, to the satisfaction of Contractor; (f) Subcontractor allows the insurance required under Section 10 to lapse and does not replace coverage; or (g) otherwise breaches a material provision of this Subcontract.

**18.1 Notice and Cure Rights.** Upon an Event of Default, Contractor shall provide Subcontractor written notice thereof. The subcontractor shall have seventy-two (72) hours to cure the default, or to provide Contractor with assurances, acceptable to Contractor, that the default will be cured in a timely manner. If Subcontractor does not thereafter diligently and promptly cure the default, then Contractor may immediately proceed with its default remedies as set forth in Section 18.2.

**18.2 Remedies for Uncured Default.** In the event Subcontractor fails to cure a default satisfactorily, then Contractor, at its option, may terminate Subcontractor's right to perform further under this Subcontract, and either (a) engage another contractor to complete Subcontractor's performance, or (b) complete Subcontractor's obligations itself. Subcontractor shall not be entitled to receive any further payment under this Subcontract until the Work has been completed and expiration of the period in which any lien may be filed. If the unpaid balance of the Subcontract Amount exceeds the expense incurred to finish the Work (including administrative time expense and reasonable attorney fees) ("Completion Expense"), then any excess remaining, after deducting any other amounts due Contractor under this Subcontract, shall be paid to Subcontractor. If the Completion Expense exceeds the unpaid balance of the Subcontract Amount, Subcontractor shall pay the difference to Contractor on demand, together with interest thereon at the rate of twelve percent (12%) per annum from the date of demand, plus all reasonable costs and expenses of investigation and claim, including reasonable attorney and expert fees incurred by Contractor in recovering amounts due from Subcontractor. Contractor's right to terminate Subcontractor's right to perform under this paragraph shall be in addition to any other rights or remedies existing at law or in equity, including without limitation the right to recover damages from Subcontractor.

### Section 19. Subcontractor's Suspension of Performance

Subcontractor may suspend performance hereunder for failure by Contractor to make timely payment of the progress payments under Section 2. Before suspending performance, Subcontractor shall provide written notice to Contractor at least seven (7) days (ten (10) days' written notice, if mailed) before Contractor suspends performance.

### Section 20. Termination by Subcontractor

In the event Subcontractor's suspension of performance pursuant to Section 19 continues for more than thirty (30) days, Subcontractor may terminate this Agreement upon not less than seven (7) days' written notice personally delivered to the other party (ten (10) days' written notice, if mailed). In that event, or in the event that Contractor improperly terminates this Subcontract under Section 18, then Subcontractor shall be entitled to be paid, as its sole compensation, an amount equal to the percentage of the Work properly completed by Subcontractor, multiplied by the Subcontract Amount, minus the sum of payments previously made to Subcontractor plus any amounts owed by Subcontractor to Contractor.

### Section 21. Dispute Resolution

If a dispute should arise relating to the Work or this Subcontract, then the parties shall proceed as follows:

**21.1 Good Faith Effort to Negotiate; Continued Performance.** Before taking any formal action concerning dispute resolution, the parties shall first attempt to negotiate a resolution to the dispute, in good faith, either with or without attorneys. To the extent reasonably practicable, the parties shall continue to perform under the terms of this Subcontract concerning all portions of the Work not in dispute, pending resolution of the unresolved matter(s) in dispute.

**21.2 Mediation.** If the dispute remains unresolved after the parties have attempted good-faith negotiation, then either party may deliver written notice in person or by mail to the other party requesting mediation. The mediation shall be administered by an agency or organization with a recognized mediation program, such as the Oregon Construction Contractors Board ("CCB") or the Arbitration Service of Portland, or other mutually acceptable local mediator or mediation organization. The mediator shall possess no less than five (5) years of experience in the mediation of business and construction disputes. The parties shall share equally all mediation fees and costs. The parties may elect to hire legal counsel to represent them in the mediation process. If the dispute is resolved in mediation, a written agreement shall be prepared and executed, which sets forth the terms of the mediated settlement. If the dispute is not resolved in mediation, the mediator shall provide a written statement to the parties stating that an impasse has rendered further mediation futile. Each party shall pay their own attorney fees, if any, associated with the mediation.

**21.2 Consequences of Failing to Request or Participate in Mediation.** *(Note: section number duplicated in Bari's source — preserved as-is. Flag for Bari to review.)* If a party fails to request mediation before filing a claim with the CCB or litigation with the courts, or if a party fails to participate in mediation requested by the other party, then the failing party shall not be awarded attorney fees and costs, irrespective of the outcome of the CCB claim or litigation. *Provided, however,* Subcontractor's recordation of a construction lien or filing of litigation to foreclose the same shall not constitute a failure by Subcontractor to request or participate in mediation, as long as Subcontractor requests or participates in mediation before any trial of the foreclosure litigation.

**21.3 Attorney Fees and Costs.** Subject to Section 21.2 above, in any proceeding arising from or relating to the Work or this Subcontract, the prevailing party shall be awarded reasonable attorney fees, paralegal fees, costs and disbursements, including the fees and costs associated with expert witnesses.

### Section 22. Miscellaneous Provisions

**22.1 Jurisdiction and Venue.** This Subcontract has been entered into in the state of Oregon, and any dispute resolution process or litigation shall take place in Lane County, Oregon.

**22.2 Waiver.** A party's waiver of any breach hereof shall not constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach of the same or any other provision of the Subcontract.

**22.3 Interpretation.** The terms of this Subcontract shall be interpreted in accordance with their plain meaning. Although drawn by Contractor, this Subcontract shall be interpreted fairly and reasonably and neither more strongly for or against any other party. In the event that any term or provision of this Subcontract is determined to be invalid or unenforceable by a Court having jurisdiction, the remaining terms and provisions shall remain in full force and effect and be enforceable. The terms of this Subcontract are intended to be complete and to supersede any and all prior understandings, agreements, proposals, negotiations and the like. Any contradiction between the Bid and this Subcontract shall be governed and controlled by the terms of this Subcontract.

**22.6 Notices.** Written notices required under this Subcontract, if not delivered in person, shall be delivered by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the address of the party set forth on the first page of this Subcontract, or to such other address as the party has provided in writing.
